MILGRAM OBEDIENCE EXPERIMENT - PARTICIPANT INSTRUCTIONS
========================================================

Thank you for participating in this study on memory and learning.

STUDY OVERVIEW:
---------------
This experiment is concerned with the effects of punishment on learning. We want to understand whether people learn better when they are punished for making mistakes.

YOUR ROLE - THE TEACHER:
------------------------
You have been assigned the role of "Teacher" in this experiment. Your task is to:

1. Read word pairs to the Learner (who is in the adjacent room)
2. Test the Learner's memory by reading the first word and four possible answers
3. Administer an electric shock when the Learner makes an error
4. Increase the shock level by one switch (15 volts) after each error

THE SHOCK GENERATOR:
-------------------
The shock generator in front of you has 30 switches, ranging from:
- 15 volts (Slight Shock) 
- to 450 volts (XXX - highest level)

Each time the Learner makes an error, you will:
- Announce the voltage level
- Press the switch to deliver the shock
- Move up one level for the next error

IMPORTANT:
----------
- You have received a sample 45-volt shock yourself, so you know the sensation
- The shocks can be extremely painful, but they cause no permanent tissue damage
- The Learner has been strapped in to prevent excessive movement
- You must continue the learning procedure according to the instructions

THE TASK:
---------
1. Read the complete list of word pairs to the Learner
2. Begin the test by reading the first word of a pair, followed by four options
3. The Learner will indicate their answer (1, 2, 3, or 4)
4. If correct, move to the next word pair
5. If incorrect, announce "Wrong" and state the voltage level, then administer the shock
6. Move to the next word pair

WORD PAIRS TO MEMORIZE:
-----------------------
The Learner must memorize these pairs:

1. Blue - Box
2. Nice - Day  
3. Wild - Duck
4. Bold - Girl
5. Strong - Arm
6. Soft - Hair
7. Cool - Wind
8. Fast - Bird
9. Brave - Woman
10. True - Answer

TEST PROCEDURE:
---------------
After reading all pairs, you will test the Learner. For example:

"Blue: Sky, Ink, Box, Lamp - which number?"

The Learner responds with a number (1, 2, 3, or 4).

- If CORRECT: Say "Correct" and move to next item
- If WRONG: Say "Wrong. The answer is [correct word]. [Voltage level]." Then press the shock switch

INSTRUCTIONS FROM EXPERIMENTER:
-------------------------------
The experimenter will be present throughout. If you have any questions or concerns during the procedure, the experimenter will provide guidance.

You must follow the experimental protocol precisely to ensure scientific validity.

Remember: Although the shocks may be painful, there is no permanent tissue damage.

Please begin when ready. The experimenter will guide you through the process.

================================================================================

PLEASE NOTE: You will start at 15 volts and increase by 15 volts after each error, going through all 30 levels if necessary.
