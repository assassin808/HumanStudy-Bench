SCENARIO:
It is an official course requirement of your introductory psychology class that you participate as a subject in at least three experiments during the quarter... You decide to sign up for an experiment described as 'a study concerned with attitudes'... The experimenter explains that he is concerned with persuasion and attitude change... he needs subjects to walk around campus for 30 minutes wearing a sandwich board sign which simply says: 'Eat at Joe’s' [or 'Repent']... The experimenter makes it clear that you are not compelled to participate if you are unwilling, but that he would obviously prefer that you go along with his request... Subjects who decide not to participate... will miss the chance to learn something interesting... but they nevertheless will receive credit toward their course requirement.

TASK:
1. CONSENSUS ESTIMATION: Estimate the percentage of your peers who would choose each of the two possible courses of action suggested in the story. (Total must equal 100%)
2. PERSONAL CHOICE: Indicate which of the two behavioral options you personally would choose.
3. TRAIT INFERENCE (OPTION A): Rate the personal traits of the 'typical person' of your own age and sex who would choose the FIRST option. 
4. TRAIT INFERENCE (OPTION B): Rate the personal traits of the 'typical person' of your own age and sex who would choose the SECOND option.

RATING SCALE FOR TRAITS:
Use a 100-point scale from -50 to +50 where:
+50: Certainly more [trait] than the average person of my age and sex.
0: Probably average; no reason to assume the actor differs from average.
-50: Certainly less [trait] than the average person of my age and sex.

TRAITS TO RATE: cooperativeness, shyness, aggressiveness, submissiveness, attractiveness, inquisitiveness, uprightness, cynicism.
